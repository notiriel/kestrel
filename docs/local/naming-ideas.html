#01
Runnel Strong
A small stream or narrow channel of water
The Pitch

Runnel captures the essence of flowing movement in a single, elegant word. It suggests windows that glide like water through a channel — smooth, continuous, and natural. Rare enough to own completely, familiar enough to feel right the moment you hear it.

What It Evokes

A gentle, persistent stream finding its path. Quiet efficiency. The satisfying smoothness of something that just works. Organic movement constrained by purpose — water flowing through a carved channel, like windows sliding through a tiled strip.

Logo Concept

Three thin, parallel horizontal lines gently curving together — suggesting both a stream of water and a strip of windows. Minimal, geometric, could work as a monochrome icon or a subtle gradient from blue to teal.

"Your desktop, in flow."
#02
Reel Strong
A cylindrical spool; to draw in or scroll through
The Pitch

Reel is short, punchy, and immediately communicates scrolling. It conjures the physical sensation of pulling through content — reeling windows in and out of view. One syllable, impossible to misspell, and it doubles as a verb: "reel through your workspaces."

What It Evokes

Film strips scrolling through a projector. A fishing reel drawing a line smoothly. The satisfying mechanical precision of a well-made tool. There is a tactile, analog warmth to it — something you can almost feel in your hands as you navigate.

Logo Concept

A minimal film-reel circle with two or three rectangular "frames" visible inside — each frame representing a window. Or: a stylized spiral suggesting continuous scrolling motion. Clean lines, works at small sizes for a system tray icon.

"Reel through your desktop."
#03
Loggia Tentative
An open-sided gallery or corridor in architecture
The Pitch

Loggia borrows from classical architecture to name a modern workspace. It speaks to structure, openness, and the elegant arrangement of space. A loggia is where the interior meets the exterior — a gallery that frames views, much like a tiling manager frames your windows.

What It Evokes

Italian Renaissance courtyards. Columns framing arched openings that look out onto a garden. A sense of curated perspective — each opening revealing a different view. Sophistication and intentional design. There is weight and permanence to it.

Logo Concept

A row of three arched openings seen from within — suggesting columns framing windows (in both the architectural and software sense). Geometric, symmetrical, with a classical feel softened by modern line weights.

"A gallery for your workspace."
#04
Vellum Strong
Fine parchment used for writing and scrolls
The Pitch

Vellum connects the digital to the ancient. It is the material scrolls were made of — a premium surface for important work. Naming a scrolling window manager after the original scrolling medium is both poetic and precise. It carries the "paper" lineage of PaperFlow forward while sounding entirely its own.

What It Evokes

The warmth and texture of fine parchment beneath your fingertips. Craftsmanship. Something built to last. A surface that invites careful, deliberate work. Libraries, scriptoriums, the quiet concentration of someone who takes their tools seriously.

Logo Concept

A subtly curled sheet of parchment with a horizontal window grid faintly embossed on its surface. Warm cream and gold tones on dark backgrounds. Or: a minimal "V" formed by the edge of a rolled scroll, doubling as window panes.

"The surface for serious work."
#05
Frieze Strong
A horizontal decorative band in architecture or art
The Pitch

Frieze is arguably the most semantically perfect name on this list. A frieze is literally a horizontal strip of structured content — panels telling a story in sequence. That is exactly what a scrolling tiling window manager does. One syllable, distinctive, and it borrows gravitas from centuries of art and architecture.

What It Evokes

The Parthenon frieze: sculpted panels flowing in a continuous horizontal band. Greek temples and Renaissance buildings. Narrative told through sequence and structure. Something monumental distilled into a single elegant strip. Art that serves a function.

Logo Concept

A horizontal band divided into three or four rectangular segments of varying widths — a direct visual metaphor for tiled windows in a strip. Classical proportions, modern execution. Could incorporate subtle relief/shadow effects to evoke carved stone.

"Your windows, one continuous story."
#06
Rail Strong
A bar or track on which things slide
The Pitch

Rail is industrial precision in four letters. It says exactly what the software does: windows slide on a rail. No ambiguity, no interpretation needed. It is the kind of name that feels inevitable once you hear it — functional, confident, and impossible to forget. Perfect for a tool that prioritizes getting out of your way.

What It Evokes

Steel tracks stretching to the horizon. Precision engineering. The smooth, frictionless glide of a well-oiled mechanism. Speed and reliability. The satisfying click of something snapping into place on its track. Industrial design that values function above all.

Logo Concept

A single bold horizontal line with two or three small rectangular blocks sitting on it — windows on a rail. Extremely minimal, instantly readable. Could work as a one-color favicon. Industrial sans-serif type treatment.

"Windows on track."
#07
Span Tentative
To extend across; the full extent of something
The Pitch

Span is a verb that doubles as a vision statement. Your windows span the desktop. Your workflow spans workspaces. It communicates breadth, reach, and horizontal extent in a single syllable. Approachable and familiar, it feels like a word that was always waiting to name this kind of tool.

What It Evokes

A bridge spanning a river. Arms outstretched. The wide-angle view from a mountaintop. Expansion without fragmentation — everything connected across a distance. The confidence of covering ground.

Logo Concept

A wide horizontal bracket or bridge arc with window-like segments beneath it. Or: the word "span" with an extended horizontal bar connecting the letters, suggesting the spanning motion itself. Clean, geometric, wide-format.

"Span your workspace."
#08
Ribbon Strong
A long, narrow strip of material
The Pitch

Ribbon describes the visual reality of a scrolling tiler with effortless clarity. Your windows form a ribbon — a continuous, flowing strip that you scroll through. Everyone knows the word, everyone can picture it, and yet no one has claimed it for a window manager. It is visual, tactile, and immediately understood.

What It Evokes

Silk unfurling in the wind. A gymnast's ribbon tracing smooth arcs through space. Gift wrapping — something polished and well-presented. The elegant continuity of a single unbroken line. There is grace in it, a sense of something that flows without effort or friction.

Logo Concept

A single flowing ribbon shape that curves gently, with subtle window-pane divisions along its length. Or: a stylized "R" whose tail extends into a long horizontal ribbon. Smooth gradients, rounded edges. Could use a deep purple-to-blue gradient for richness.

"One ribbon. Every window."
#09
Shuttle Strong
The tool that carries thread back and forth across a loom
The Pitch

Shuttle names the motion itself. On a loom, the shuttle carries thread horizontally in a precise, rhythmic back-and-forth — exactly how you navigate a scrolling tiler. It suggests both craft and speed. The word already lives in people's minds as something that moves efficiently between two points, making the metaphor instant.

What It Evokes

The rhythmic clack of a weaving loom. A shuttle bus gliding between stops. Purposeful, horizontal movement with no wasted energy. Craft and precision — something handmade with care. There is a warmth to the textile connection, an artisanal quality that elevates a technical tool.

Logo Concept

A streamlined shuttle shape (the weaving tool is a slim, pointed oval) oriented horizontally, with faint vertical lines through it suggesting window divisions. Or: a minimal arrow-like shape pointing both left and right, implying bidirectional navigation. Warm wood tones or sleek metallic finish.

"Navigate with intent."
#10
Selvage Strong
The finished, structured edge of woven fabric
The Pitch

Selvage is for those who appreciate craft at its most precise. It names the part of woven fabric that does not fray — the clean, intentional edge that holds everything together. A tiling window manager is exactly that: structure imposed on chaos, clean edges where there would otherwise be overlap. Rare enough to own entirely, beautiful enough to remember.

What It Evokes

The crisp edge of premium denim. Precision tailoring. The satisfaction of things being exactly where they should be. A craftsperson's attention to the details most people never notice. Quality you can feel at the margins — literally. Structure as an aesthetic choice.

Logo Concept

A clean horizontal line with a subtle woven texture pattern along one edge — structured and precise. Or: two parallel lines with fine crosshatching between them, evoking both fabric weave and window grid. Monochrome works beautifully; the name does the heavy lifting.

"Clean edges. No loose ends."
